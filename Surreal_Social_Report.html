Social Media Performance Report

Instagram performance for Michelle Marsh's individual account and the Surreal Hair and Beauty business account, prepared from the analytics captures supplied in July 2026.

About this report. The figures below are drawn from seventeen analytics screenshots supplied from the Instagram professional dashboard. Two distinct accounts appear in the material. The first is a large, high-reach account whose content centres on education, touring, and personal profile, including the Shades ALK Tour, the scalp bleaching reel, and the In Business with Michelle Marsh feature. The second is a smaller local account of around 350 followers with steady month-on-month growth. On the balance of the content, the larger account has been treated as Michelle Marsh's individual account and the smaller as the Surreal Hair and Beauty business account.

The account handles were not visible in the captures, so this attribution should be confirmed before the report is shared more widely. Reporting periods differ between the two accounts because the captures were taken from different views. Michelle's figures cover roughly the four months from mid-March to mid-July 2026. The Surreal figures combine calendar-month summaries with rolling ninety-day and thirty-day views, each noted where it appears.

Michelle Marsh, individual account

The pattern is that of an account whose reels are being surfaced widely in discovery rather than consumed only by an existing audience, which is the profile of an educator building professional profile beyond the salon's local base.

The two accounts side by side

How the accounts compare

The accounts play different roles and should be read against different expectations. Michelle's account is a profile and reach engine, pushing educational content into a national and international professional audience. The Surreal account is a local relationship channel, smaller in scale but with a loyal and growing base that engages more consistently. The clearest opportunity sits in the connection between the two, since the strongest recent result on the Surreal account came from a piece of Michelle's content.

The link between the accounts is the biggest untapped lever

Michelle's account reaches tens of thousands of non-followers each month. The Surreal account's single best recent month was driven by one shared feature. A deliberate handover, where a portion of Michelle's reach is directed to Surreal, would convert profile into local following far faster than the Surreal account can achieve alone.

Recommendations

What to do next

The recommendations below are ordered by likely return relative to effort, and are written to fit the tools already in use across the engagement, including Timely, Klaviyo and the salon's booking flow. Each is intended to be actioned without new platforms or significant cost.

Priority one

Direct Michelle's reach towards the Surreal account

The scalp bleaching and salon-day reels reached well over one hundred thousand accounts with only single-digit new follows on Michelle's own account, which shows how much attention is passing through without capture. Where a reel features the salon, tag the Surreal account clearly, add a spoken or on-screen prompt to follow Surreal for booking, and pin a comment linking to the Surreal profile. Even a small conversion rate from Michelle's monthly non-follower reach would double the Surreal following within a quarter.

Priority two

Convert social reach into bookings and email subscribers

Reach is currently ending at the view rather than moving into the booking or email pipeline. Add a single, consistent link in both profiles that leads to the Timely booking page and to a short email sign-up managed in Klaviyo. When a reel performs unusually well, follow it within a day with a story or post that carries the booking prompt, so that attention is captured while it is still live. This connects the social effort to the salon's existing five thousand five hundred client database rather than leaving it as a vanity metric.

Priority three

Post the Surreal account into its strongest window

Surreal's audience is most active on Monday, Tuesday and Wednesday evenings between six and nine. Schedule the most important post of each week into that window, and use the quieter days for lighter content. A simple weekly rhythm, planned in advance, will lift early reach without requiring more content overall.

Priority four

Make stories work harder on the Surreal account

Stories currently account for barely one and a half per cent of Surreal's interactions, yet they are the most natural place for booking prompts, polls and behind-the-scenes salon content. A short daily story sequence, three or four frames, gives the account a consistent presence between posts and creates repeated opportunities to prompt a booking or a follow.

Priority five

Repeat what already works

The content that carried both accounts was concrete and human, being salon days, real education, and a genuine feature about the business. Themed reels shot inside the salon, short technical explainers, and client transformation pieces are the formats to schedule more of. Lean into the salon-day and education formats that drew the largest reach, and treat abstract or purely promotional posts as secondary.
