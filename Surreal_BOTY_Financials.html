Growth in Sales Figures · 20% of entry

Six-year turnover growth

Turnover has risen from $842,543 in 2020 to $1,744,250 in 2025, an increase of 107 per cent. Growth has been steady rather than reliant on any single year, with the largest step in 2022 and a consolidation year in 2025 as the salon absorbed significant team changes.

Growth in Sales Figures · resilience

Team changes absorbed in 2025

The recent year was one of significant team change. Six team members left, together accounting for $421,863 of stylist-generated revenue in their final year, most of them long-serving. That the business still grew its turnover while absorbing this loss is the clearest evidence that revenue is now carried by systems rather than by individuals. The majority of these clients were retained and transitioned to other stylists.

Growth in Sales Figures · profitability

Revenue and cost structure

Surreal returned a company profit of $339,008 in the recent year, 19.4 per cent of turnover and well above the industry benchmark. This comprises reported net profit together with the shareholder salaries drawn by the owners. Measured before those salaries, net profit is $264,875, or 14.9 per cent of revenue, which still sits at the upper end of the trade's healthy range. The cost structure is shown from two views below.
